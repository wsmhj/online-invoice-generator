What Should Every Invoice Include?

A professional invoice is more than just a payment request — it is a legal document that protects both you and your client. Making sure every invoice is complete reduces disputes and helps you get paid faster.

Required Invoice Elements

  • Your business name, address, and contact information
  • Client's name, address, and contact information
  • A unique invoice number for easy tracking
  • Invoice date and payment due date
  • Detailed description of services or products
  • Quantity and unit price for each line item
  • Subtotal, applicable taxes, and total amount due
  • Accepted payment methods and bank details
  • Late payment policy or penalty terms

Tips to Get Paid Faster

  • Send invoices immediately after completing work
  • Use clear, specific descriptions for each line item
  • Set a specific due date (not "Net 30" without the actual date)
  • Offer multiple payment methods (bank transfer, card, PayPal)
  • Follow up with a friendly reminder 3 days before the due date
  • Include a late payment fee in your payment terms
  • Keep a consistent invoice number format for easy reference
  • Always get a purchase order number if working with businesses

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about creating and sending invoices.

How do I save my invoice as a PDF? ▼
Click the "Print" button and select "Save as PDF" (or "Microsoft Print to PDF" on Windows) as your printer destination. This creates a professional PDF of your invoice that you can email or save to your computer. On Mac, you can also use File > Print > PDF.
Can I add my logo to the invoice? ▼

What's the difference between an invoice and a receipt? ▼
An invoice is sent before payment to request money owed. It outlines what services or products were provided and the amount due. A receipt is issued after payment has been made as proof of the transaction. Both are important business documents, but they serve different purposes in the billing cycle.
How should I number my invoices? ▼
A common format is to include the year and a sequential number, such as INV-2025-001, INV-2025-002, etc. This makes invoices easy to track, search, and reference in communications with clients. Some businesses also include a client code, like INV-ACME-001.
Do I need to include taxes on my invoice? ▼
Tax requirements vary by country, state, and type of business. In the US, businesses typically collect sales tax if selling tangible goods or certain services. Freelancers are generally not required to charge sales tax on services in most states. Always check your local tax regulations or consult an accountant to ensure compliance.
